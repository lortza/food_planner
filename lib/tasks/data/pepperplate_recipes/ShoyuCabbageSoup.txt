Title: Shoyu Cabbage Soup
Description: Humble cabbage takes center stage in this vegetarian ramen inspired soup.
Source: Bon Appétit
Original URL: http://www.bonappetit.com/recipe/shoyu-cabbage-soup
Yield: 4
Active: 15
Total: 50 min
Categories: soup, cabbage, chopsticks
Image: https://cdn2.pepperplate.com/recipes/011909f7e14545eeb870bb255e04ebc0.jpg
Ingredients:
	1 medium onion, thinly sliced
	2 carrots, peeled, thinly sliced or peeled into ribbons
	2 celery stalks, thinly sliced
	2 garlic cloves, chopped
	1/2 head Napa cabbage, chopped <-- 1 qt bag of thawed frozen cabbage
	4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
	2 tablespoons (or more) shoyu (Japanese soy sauce)
	2 tablespoons unseasoned rice vinegar
	2 teaspoons sugar
	2 cups cooked soba noodles <-- 2 packages of ramen noodles
	1/4 bunch of cilantro
	Hot chili paste (such as sambal oelek) and fresh cilantro leaves (for serving)

[Ingredient info]
	Shoyu and hot chili paste can be found at Asian markets and in the Asian foods section of many supermarkets.

Instructions:
[Noodles]
	In a smaller pot, cook the ramen noodles

[Veg & Broth]
	In the soup pot, sautee the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic until softened
	Add cabbage, broth, shoyu, vinegar, and sugar
	Bring to a boil, then back down to simmer until vegetables are very tender, 15-20 minutes (cover partially with a lid)
	Season to taste

[Assemble]
	Drain the noodles, serve them into bowls
	Pour soup on top of noodles
	Add cilantro.
